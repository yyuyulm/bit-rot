"#000000" Square is a mutable generative painting that explores decay and aging in a digital ecosystem. Inspired by the colorful under layers revealed through decay and cracking in Kazimir Malevich's Black Square painting, #000000 square begins as a black square sized 80 * 80 pixels (displayed as 79.5 * 79.5, in accordance with the dimension of Black Square in centimeters), which are encoded as 153600 zero bits, (80*80 pixels * 3 color channels * 8 bits per color value) and displayed through bitmapping. Every day, a random bit would flip to its opposite (named bit-rot in digital storage), resulting in a color change of the corresponding pixel, adding artifacts of decaying to the painting. At the sametime, the randomization algorithm used is intentionally computationally expensive and increases the load time of the painting as it ages. The initial date of the painting is set to December 9, 1968, the date of the demonstration of the oN-Line System (NSL), which is the basis of modern personal computers and the first commercial prototype that implements bitmapping for image display. The project is written in HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript and hosted through GitHub Pages.
